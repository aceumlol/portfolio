Fearless Points System

View Project

When LEC introduced Fearless mode, I was interested in the new drafting challenges. It got me thinking: how could we truly spot the most "OP" champion in this new meta?

Some sites were already showing their "Prioscore" system, which was a good start. But I felt like this approach was too generalized.

My thinking was, why not value the context of the pick?
• Blind picks are theoretically stronger than counter picks
• B1 - blind pick is a power move and deserves way more weight than normal blind
• While R5 - counter pick is the ultimate counter and also needs its own unique value
• The same logic we can apply to R3
• And obviously, 1st phase bans are more global based than role-aiming 2nd phase bans

Of course, I know that every team has its own drafting biases and no system can be perfect. But I'm confident that my results are more dynamic and accurate than in any generalized system.

SoloQ Tracker

Automated dashboard with team's SoloQ performance


Key Features:
• Auto-refreshes every 5 minutes
• Weekly Team Recap with a quick summary of the team's week - current ranks, and champion recap
• Player pages with an option to display data as Match History, Summary or Match Ups
• Table summarizing all-champion stats and comparing it to average opponent performance
• Option to switch between Farming or Utility based stats

Matchup & Synergy Database

This tool lets you search through all games to check statistics (WR%/Games) for specific draft combinations. It shows all champion pairings related to given champion(s) and allows you to access the VOD when filtering for individual games.

Based on Tier 1 games since 2023.

Drafting Tool

A tool for analyzing historical game drafts. It allows to input one or more champions into any pick/ban position to find similar or identical drafts. You can adjust a similarity setting to control how closely the results match your query.

Additionally, second tab 'Phases' shows how a specific pick influences every subsequent choice in the draft.

Task App

I've always been using Notion/Google to keep track of my tasks, but I realized they weren't quite what I needed. I wanted full control - customizable tags, adjustable columns (and their contents), and even the ability to change color palettes. But none of the existing pre-made apps gave me all that flexibility.

So, I decided to build my own App, featuring all these options and even more.

I like adapting and finding solutions when the existing ones don't fit - even if it means spending countless hours learning completely new things.
